Introduction to Solving a Rubik's Cube

Notation

Notation is very important when learning to solve a Rubik's Cube because you must now how to read algorithms off of websites as you are learning the steps to solving the rubiks cube. There are many different notations used by different people, especially as you move on to bigger cubes. However, for the 3x3x3 the notation is pretty standard. Rubiks cube notation essentially assigns each face of the cube a letter, and then modifies those letters to represent moves (turns).

Here is an image of the names of each face of the rubiks cube:

Now that you know the names of each face, the move names should follow pretty simply. A letter on its own represents turning that letters face clockwise. And a letter with an apostophe represents turning the letters corresponding face counterclockwise. Note, the clockwise/counterclockwise turns are as if you are looking at the face straight on.

Solving the Rubiks Cube

A common misconception that people have about solving the rubik's cube is that you solve the cube side by side. This is is not efficient and most people do not do that. Instead, most rubik's cube solvers solve the cube layer by layer. In this website, you will learn tosolve the cube in the simplest, yet still impresive way. In this method, you will solve the first layer, then move on to filling in the second layer, and then finally move on to working your way through the last layer.

Step 1: The white Cross

Creating white cross is the first step in turning your cube from a scrambled mess to a nice, orderly, solved state. This step is often considered the most difficult step of solving the rubiks cube because this is the only section of solving the rubiks cube that doesn't have algorithms (sort-of), so it is the most reliant on human creativity and ingenuity.
